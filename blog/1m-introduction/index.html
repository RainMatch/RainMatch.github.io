The $1M Introduction

How one warm intro can change a company's entire revenue trajectory

The $1M Party

I used to go to many tradeshows, mainly to catch up with industry friends and make new ones. A lot of parties, hosted by larger companies at fancy places. We had no budget for such but always wanted to do one with our name on it. I decided, what the heck, I'll just try a small, friends-only, private party — spend my money just enough so it wouldn't break my bank in case I can't expense it.

I realized afterwards that people were fed up with huge, noisy parties and ours was perfect to relax and have private conversations. Friends brought their friends, texted others: "Hey you should come here now." As the host, I got introduced to so many new people, had real conversations, and 3 months later, closed a few deals worth about $1M ARR.

Think of the large, fancy parties as mass cold outreach — and the small, private ones as warm intros.

It All Starts with a Simple Question

Most people underestimate the value of a single introduction. They think deals are built through cold emails, outbound campaigns, and long sales processes.

But in reality, some of the biggest deals start with something much simpler:

"Do you know anyone at XYZ?"

One introduction. That's all it takes. The right introduction can:

  • Open doors that were previously inaccessible
  • Accelerate trust instantly
  • Collapse months of sales cycles into weeks

In many cases, that one introduction is worth more than an entire pipeline of cold leads. I've seen deals where a single connection led to enterprise contracts, long-term partnerships, and millions in revenue.

That's why I call it The $1M Introduction.

These numbers reveal something most sales strategies overlook: the highest-leverage moment in a deal is not the pitch — it's the introduction.

When a deal closes nearly 70% faster, trust is already established before the first real conversation even begins. Conversion rates are significantly higher because the buyer is not starting from zero. A referral acts as borrowed credibility, reducing skepticism and increasing openness.

And the influence of peer recommendations shows that buying decisions are not purely rational — they are deeply social. People rely on trusted voices to reduce risk, especially in high-stakes B2B environments.

This changes how we should think about sales. The real bottleneck is not outreach. It's access and trust. And the most efficient way to solve both is through the right introduction.

That's why one introduction can be worth more than thousands of cold emails.

Three Practical Tactics

1 Identify Your "High-Leverage Connectors"

Not all contacts are equal. Some people in your network have access to decision-makers, strong credibility in their industry, and influence over buying decisions.

Focus on identifying these individuals. These are the people who can create $1M introductions.

2 Make Introductions Before Asking for Them

The fastest way to receive valuable introductions is to give them first. Connect people who can help each other, share opportunities proactively, and become known as a trusted connector.

Over time, this builds trust, reciprocity, and network strength. And when you need an introduction, people are far more willing to help.
