Getting Started with RainMatch

Whether you're a business owner looking for help closing deals or a seasoned professional wanting to monetize your network, RainMatch is built to make that first connection simple. This guide walks you through everything you need to get up and running.

Step 1: Download the App

RainMatch is available on both iOS and Android. Head to the App Store or Google Play to install it. The app is free.

Step 2: Create Your Profile

Your profile is the first thing potential matches see. A strong profile significantly increases the quality of matches you receive. Here's what to focus on:

  • Professional headline — describe who you are and what you bring to the table in one sentence.
  • Industry and expertise — be specific. The matching algorithm relies heavily on this to surface relevant opportunities.
  • Your role — indicate whether you're primarily a Seller, a Partner, or both.
  • Deal preferences — set expectations for deal size, commission range, and the types of products or markets you work with.

Tip: Users with complete profiles receive significantly better matches. Take 5 minutes to fill in every section before searching for matches.

Step 3: Post a Project (Sellers)

If you're a Seller, your next step is to post a Project. A Project is your pitch to potential Partners — it describes what you're selling, who the ideal customer is, and what you're offering in return.

A compelling project listing includes:

  1. A clear description of your product or service
  2. Your Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) — industry, company size, geography
  3. Commission structure — percentage of revenue, profit, or a fixed fee per closed deal
  4. Any relevant materials or context a Partner would need to represent you

Step 4: Browse and Like Matches

RainMatch will begin surfacing matches once your profile and projects are live. New matches are curated daily — not a firehose, but a focused set of relevant connections.

As a Seller, you'll see suggested Partner profiles. Review their background, expertise, and network focus. Like the ones you want to work with.

As a Partner, you'll see Projects that match your expertise. Review each one carefully — the commission offer, the Seller's track record, and whether the product fits your network. Like the projects you're interested in.

Step 5: Connect and Start Collaborating

When both sides like each other, a connection is formed and a chat opens automatically. Use this to align on expectations, share context, and plan how you'll approach potential customers together.

Tip: The first message sets the tone. Be specific about why you're excited about the opportunity and what you bring to the collaboration.
